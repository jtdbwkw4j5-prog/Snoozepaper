Headline: Supreme Court rules IEEPA does not authorize president to impose tariffs

Date of event: 2026‑02‑20 | Date logged: 2026‑06‑04 | Location: Washington, D.C.
On February 20 2026 the U.S. Supreme Court consolidated two cases and held that the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) does not authorize the president to impose tariffs. The Court vacated and remanded one case and affirmed another, concluding that tariff authority remains with Congress. The syllabus explains that the Constitution gives Congress, not the president, the power to levy tariffs.
