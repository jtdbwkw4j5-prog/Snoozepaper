Today’s Ledger — June 23 2026

Below are fact-only news ledger entries for June 23 2026. These summaries state who was involved, what happened, when and where, and the source of the information. No editorializing or opinion is included.

IMO announces Strait of Hormuz evacuation plan

Date of event: 2026-06-23 | Date logged: 2026-06-24 | Location: Strait of Hormuz / Persian Gulf region
The International Maritime Organization announced an evacuation plan for more than 11,000 seafarers stranded in the Gulf region. The IMO said the operation would be carried out with Iran, Oman, other coastal states, the United States and maritime industry partners after safety guarantees were secured for navigation.
Source(s): International Maritime Organization

UN commission says Israeli forces targeted Palestinian children; Israel rejects findings

Date of event: 2026-06-23 | Date logged: 2026-06-24 | Location: Gaza Strip / occupied Palestinian territory / Israel
A United Nations independent commission said Israeli authorities and security forces deliberately targeted Palestinian children, resulting in genocide and other atrocity crimes in Gaza and war crimes in the West Bank. Israel rejected the commission’s findings and has disputed the allegations.
Source(s): United Nations Office at Geneva

U.S. Senate passes Iran war powers resolution

Date of event: 2026-06-23 | Date logged: 2026-06-24 | Location: Washington, D.C., United States
The United States Senate voted 50-48 to pass a war powers resolution directing President Donald Trump to halt military operations against Iran. Reuters reported that the Senate action followed a similar House vote and marked a congressional rebuke of the president’s Iran policy.
Source(s): Reuters

Police say attack in Nigeria’s Plateau state killed 20 people

Date of event: 2026-06-23 | Date logged: 2026-06-24 | Location: Bokkos district, Plateau state, Nigeria
Police in Nigeria’s central Plateau state said 20 people were killed in a weekend attack on a community in Bokkos district. Police said officers and other security agencies engaged the attackers before they fled. Eighteen people were reported killed at the scene and two later died in hospital.
Source(s): Reuters via MarketScreener

France records hottest day as heatwave brings nearly 40 drownings

Date of event: 2026-06-23 | Date logged: 2026-06-24 | Location: France / Western Europe
France recorded its hottest day on record during an early summer heatwave affecting much of Western Europe. The Associated Press reported that nearly 40 people drowned in France over the previous week, many while seeking relief in water during the heatwave.
Source(s): Associated Press
