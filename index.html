What SnoozePaper Does

SnoozePaper summarizes public events using only verifiable information: who was involved, what happened, when it happened, where it happened and where the information came from. Each summary is logged plainly so you can read the record without amplification or interpretation.

What SnoozePaper Does Not Do

We do not publish opinion pieces, predictions or commentary. We avoid outrage framing, emotional adjectives and algorithmic personalization. We do not invent context, reproduce full articles or copy from paywalled sources. We do not publish obituaries (only death announcements when relevant) and we never provide weather forecasts.

The Standard

SnoozePaper is built around constraint. Each entry follows a strict format and is logged without amplification. The goal is to reduce interpretive pressure: you read the record, sleep on it and decide for yourself.

Launch Note

SnoozePaper is now live in early public development. Today’s Ledger is being tested as a fact-only public record: who, what, when, where and sources. The format will be refined over time, but the core rule remains the same — no outrage, no forecasts, no emotional framing. Just the record.
